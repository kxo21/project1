Derrick Deshaun Watson, star QB for the Houston Texans was born September 14, 1995 in Gainesville Georgia.

College Career

He attended Clemnson University for 3 years where he threw for 90 TD's on just over 10,000 yards with a 67.4% completion percentage. He also rushed for 1,934 yards while adding 26 TD's on the ground.

In 2015, his sophmore season at Clemson Watson had over 4k yards passing with 35 touchdowns. He also added 1,100 yards rushing with 12 rushing touchdwons. He led the Tigers to a 12-0 regular season finsish ranking them #1 overall seed in the nation. They faced off against North Carolina in the ACC Championship Game, defeating the Tar Heals 45-37. Clemson defeated went on to beat the OK Sooners in the Orange Bowl and finally faced Alabama in the championship game. Watson threw for 400 yards, 4 TD's, and ran for 73 yards to lose 45-40. That same year, Watson was named the 2015 ACC Player of the Year and ACC Offensive Player of the Year and finished third in Heisman voting.

The following year Watson was even better statistically. He finished the season with 4,593 yards passing and 41 passing touchdowns, in addition to 629 yards rushing and 9 rushing touchdowns. He followed an identical route to the Natinal Championship defeating Ohio State 31-0 in the Fiesta Bowl. Watson had a subpar passing game, going for 259 yards, 1 TD to go along with 2 interceptions. He did add 2 rushing touchdowns on 15 for 57 rushing. Watson was able to obtain his redemption against the Alabama Roll Tide. Watson was stellar against the nations top defense, completing 36-of-56 passes for 402 yards a 3 TD's.
