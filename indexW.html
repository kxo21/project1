DERRICK DESHAUN WATSON

He attended Clemnson University for 3 years where he threw for 90 TD's on just over 10,000 yards with a 67.4% completion percentage. He also rushed for 1,934 yards while adding 26 TD's on the ground.

In 2015, his sophmore season at Clemson Watson had over 4k yards passing with 35 touchdowns. He also added 1,100 yards rushing with 12 rushing touchdwons. He led the Tigers to a 12-0 regular season finsish ranking them #1 overall seed in the nation. They faced off against North Carolina in the ACC Championship Game, defeating the Tar Heals 45-37. Clemson defeated went on to beat the OK Sooners in the Orange Bowl and finally faced Alabama in the College Football Championship Game. Watson threw for 400 yards, 4 TD's, and ran for 73 yards to lose 45-40. That same year, Watson was named the 2015-2016 ACC Player of the Year and ACC Offensive Player of the Year and finished third in Heisman voting.

The following year Watson was even better statistically. He finished the season with 4,593 yards passing and 41 passing touchdowns, in addition to 629 yards rushing and 9 rushing touchdowns. He followed an identical route to the Natinal Championship defeating Ohio State 31-0 in the Fiesta Bowl. Watson had a subpar passing game, going for 259 yards, 1 TD to go along with 2 interceptions. He did add 2 rushing touchdowns on 15 for 57 rushing. Watson was able to obtain his redemption against the Alabama Roll Tide. Watson was stellar against the nations top defense, completing 36-of-56 passes for 402 yards and 3 TD's.

Watson was drafted 12th overall by the Houston Texans in the 2017 NFL Draft. Many expected Watson to be a first round pick, but not many expected him to drop as far down as 12 on the list. Watson's mix of speed, arm strength, vision, and magician-like plays made while in college was sure to land him in the top 5 of his draft class. In his first game as a Texan against the Jacksonville Jaguars, Watson threw for 102 yards and a TD in just 2 quarters of play afte Tom Savage was benched. From that moment on Watson went on to become the future franchise QB the Texans have longed be hoping for. In just his first season he played 7 games on 6 starts, finishing with 1700 passing yards, 19 TD's, an 8 interceptions. Also providing 269 rushing yards and 2 rushing TD's. In his second season as the Texans full-time playcaller he finished with 4,165 passing yards, 26 TD's, 9 INT's, 551 rushing yards and 5 rushing TD's, leading the Texans to #1 seed in the AFC South Division and a first round playoff birth. 2019 being the most recent season, Watson finshied just shy of 4k yards with 3,852 and 26 passint TD's and 12 INT's. He lead the Texans to the second round of the playoffs.
