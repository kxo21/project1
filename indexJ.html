LAMAR DEMEATRICE JACKSON JR.

Lamar Jackson Jr. attended Univ. of Louisville for 3 years where he threw for a total of 9,043 yards and 69 TD's; along with making a name for himself in the running game where he ran for a total of 4,132 yards and scored 50 rushing TD's.

In his freshman season at Louisville, Jackson appeard in 12 games and completed 1,840 passing yards, 960 rushing yards, and 12 and 11 TD's respectively.

The following season as a sophomore he came in blazing by setting a Louisville record for most TD's (8) in a game with all of them coming in the first half. In the game against #2 ranked Florida St. Jackson put himself in the Heisman rankings by defeating them and completing 146 rushing yards and 4 rushing TD's. He also thre for 216 yards and 1 passing TD to go along with his stellar performance. Jackson also played against Watson and the Clemson Tigers, where he he had a toal of 586 offensive yards - 295 passing and 162 rushing as well as 3 total touchdowns scored. To complete the year, Jackson Jr was awarded the 2016 Heisman Trophy winner to become Louisville's first Heisman winner ever and the youngest player to win the award, period.

In his third and final season at the University of Louisville, Jackson played in a total of 13 games and completed 3,660 passing yards, 27 touchdowns and had 10 interceptions. At the conclusion of the season he was named ACC Athlete of the Year and also finished the season 3rd in Hesiman voting.

January 2018, Jackson entered the NFL draft and was selected #32 overall by the Baltimore Ravens
